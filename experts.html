EXPERTS

At Bibhuti Bista - Research and Consulting Services, we collaborate with a network of highly experienced and dedicated experts across various fields. Our team of experts brings a wealth of knowledge and a commitment to excellence, ensuring that we provide the highest quality services to our clients.

Our experts are chosen for their specialized skills and their ability to provide practical, data-driven solutions. We work closely with government agencies, development partners, and NGOs to deliver impactful research, evaluations, and strategic consulting services.

Malen Tuon, Cambodian

Malen, brings over 15 years of professional experience across education, health, agriculture, and community development, with strong expertise in project implementation, donor compliance, and operational management. She has actively supported research and data collection, including field surveys, interviews, transcription, synthesis, and analysis to produce actionable insights and impact narratives. Malen possesses specialized skills in gender equity, disability inclusion, and participatory evaluation methodologies, combining qualitative and quantitative research proficiency. Her extensive knowledge of Cambodia’s socio-economic and education context, engagement with government institutions, local authorities, schools, and marginalized communities, enables her to contribute effectively to data quality assurance, field coordination, and evidence-based reporting. Malen’s experience ensures rigorous, culturally sensitive, and actionable evaluation support.

Sing Sophea, Cambodian

He brings over 20 years of professional experience in monitoring, evaluation, research, and learning (MERL), with strong sectoral expertise in education, gender equality and disability inclusion (GEDSI), youth development, health, governance, and community-based programming. He has contributed to multiple external evaluations and learning-focused reviews of donor-funded programs in Cambodia, applying Theory of Change–based approaches, mixed-methods analysis, and participatory evaluation techniques. His expertise includes integrating GEDSI into evaluation design, tools, sampling, and analysis, ensuring inclusive participation of girls, children with disabilities, and other marginalized groups. With deep knowledge of Cambodia’s education system, local policies, institutions, and community dynamics, he is well-positioned to support rigorous, culturally sensitive, and evidence-based evaluation processes that generate actionable recommendations for inclusive development programs.

Bharat Prasad Nepal, Nepalese

Bharat Prasad Nepal is a senior development practitioner with over 25 years of experience in agriculture, livelihoods, and community development in Nepal, focusing on improving the socio-economic status of smallholder farmers, women, and marginalized groups through inclusive and sustainable strategies. He has expertise in value chain and market development, participatory market system development (PMSD), enterprise promotion, and food security, working with donors and partners like USAID, JICA, ILO, DFID/UKAID, and iDE Nepal. Mr. Nepal has led project design, research, field supervision, stakeholder coordination, and capacity building, and has trained practitioners, government officials, and community actors in market systems, gender-responsive, and nutrition-sensitive approaches. Committed to inclusive growth and resilient livelihoods, he integrates research, practice, and leadership to create lasting development impact.

Er. Rupendra Basnet, Nepalese

Er. Rupendra Basnet is a seasoned disaster risk reduction (DRR) professional with over two decades of experience leading and advising on resilience, preparedness, and post-disaster recovery initiatives across Nepal. Since 2005, he has worked with national and international NGOs, UN agencies, and as a consultant, designing and managing donor-funded programs focused on inclusive DRR, climate change adaptation, early warning and early action systems, and community- and school-based disaster risk management. He has served as Program/Project Manager and Team Leader for major earthquake and flood response, risk reduction, and reconstruction programs, with a strong emphasis on safer, barrier-free, and climate-smart infrastructure. His expertise spans seismic safety, strengthening emergency response capacity from the community to the national level, and applied research on inclusion and preparedness.

Sutapa Chakma, Bangladeshi

Sutapa brings a strong blend of protection expertise, research capability, and ethical sensitivity that makes her a valuable asset to the team. With experience rooted in gender-based violence programming and close collaboration with child protection actors, she demonstrates deep understanding of children’s rights, safeguarding principles, PSEA, and safe referral pathways. Her research background—including a feminist qualitative study on economic violence against Indigenous women—shows her ability to design and manage rigorous, intersectional studies grounded in human rights analysis. Sutapa’s proven ability to work effectively with NGOs, government institutions and community actors reflects her strong facilitation and communication skills, backed by a trauma-informed and survivor-centered mindset. Her advanced analytical and technical writing skills, strengthened through a Master’s in Human Rights in Europe, ensure that she produces high-quality, well-structured reports. Importantly, her consistent adherence to ethical research standards and commitment to confidentiality, informed consent, and do-no-harm make her particularly well suited to work with vulnerable groups, including children and survivors.

Ananya Dasgupta, Indian

With nearly three decades of direct engagement in child rights, legal research, and policy analysis, A. Dasgupta brings exceptional depth and credibility to the team. Their expertise spans child protection, sexual exploitation, and trafficking, built on a strong academic foundation and extensive work with NGOs, international organizations, and government stakeholders. Dasgupta has a distinguished track record managing and conducting complex qualitative research and evaluations across India, including major assignments with Plan International, Terre des Hommes, Enfold, and APPI. Their work incorporates participatory methodologies such as outcome harvesting, appreciative inquiry, and most significant change, demonstrating methodological rigor and adaptability. Years of facilitating life-skills, personal safety, and gender-sensitivity sessions with children and adults have refined their communication skills and trauma-informed approach. Known for strong analytical abilities, high-quality English report writing, and unwavering commitment to ethical research practice, Dasgupta brings both technical excellence and a deeply empathetic, child-centered lens to the team.
