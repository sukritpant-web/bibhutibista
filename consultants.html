CONSULTANTS

At Bibhuti Bista - Research and Consulting Services, we collaborate with a network of highly experienced and dedicated consultants across various fields. Our team of experts brings a wealth of knowledge and a commitment to excellence, ensuring that we provide the highest quality services to our clients.

Our consultants are chosen for their specialized skills and their ability to provide practical, data-driven solutions. We work closely with government agencies, development partners, and NGOs to deliver impactful research, evaluations, and strategic consulting services.

Malen Tuon, Cambodian

Malen, brings over 15 years of professional experience across education, health, agriculture, and community development, with strong expertise in project implementation, donor compliance, and operational management. She has actively supported research and data collection, including field surveys, interviews, transcription, synthesis, and analysis to produce actionable insights and impact narratives. Malen possesses specialized skills in gender equity, disability inclusion, and participatory evaluation methodologies, combining qualitative and quantitative research proficiency. Her extensive knowledge of Cambodia’s socio-economic and education context, engagement with government institutions, local authorities, schools, and marginalized communities, enables her to contribute effectively to data quality assurance, field coordination, and evidence-based reporting. Malen’s experience ensures rigorous, culturally sensitive, and actionable evaluation support.

Sing Sophea, Cambodian

He brings over 20 years of professional experience in monitoring, evaluation, research, and learning (MERL), with strong sectoral expertise in education, gender equality and disability inclusion (GEDSI), youth development, health, governance, and community-based programming. He has contributed to multiple external evaluations and learning-focused reviews of donor-funded programs in Cambodia, applying Theory of Change–based approaches, mixed-methods analysis, and participatory evaluation techniques. His expertise includes integrating GEDSI into evaluation design, tools, sampling, and analysis, ensuring inclusive participation of girls, children with disabilities, and other marginalized groups. With deep knowledge of Cambodia’s education system, local policies, institutions, and community dynamics, he is well-positioned to support rigorous, culturally sensitive, and evidence-based evaluation processes that generate actionable recommendations for inclusive development programs.

Bharat Prasad Nepal, Nepalese

Bharat Prasad Nepal is a senior development practitioner with over 25 years of experience in agriculture, livelihoods, and community development in Nepal, focusing on improving the socio-economic status of smallholder farmers, women, and marginalized groups through inclusive and sustainable strategies. He has expertise in value chain and market development, participatory market system development (PMSD), enterprise promotion, and food security, working with donors and partners like USAID, JICA, ILO, DFID/UKAID, and iDE Nepal. Mr. Nepal has led project design, research, field supervision, stakeholder coordination, and capacity building, and has trained practitioners, government officials, and community actors in market systems, gender-responsive, and nutrition-sensitive approaches. Committed to inclusive growth and resilient livelihoods, he integrates research, practice, and leadership to create lasting development impact.
